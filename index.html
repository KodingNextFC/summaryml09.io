What is a Recommendation System?

  • A system that suggests items (movies, songs, products, videos) that best match a user's interests.
  • Its goal: help users find relevant things among millions of options without searching one by one.
  • The more a user interacts with it, the "smarter" the system gets at recognizing their taste.
Example: watch suggestions on Netflix, the "Discover" playlist on Spotify, or "You Might Like" on an online store.

What is Unsupervised Learning?

  • A way of training machine learning models without labels or predefined correct answers.
  • The model finds hidden patterns and groups in the data on its own, a process known as clustering.
  • In recommendation systems, this technique is used to group similar users or items without being told the categories beforehand.
Example: grouping online shoppers into a few "buyer types" purely from their shopping habits, with no labels defined by a human.

What is Collaborative Filtering?

  • Recommends items based on the behavior of other users who are similar to you.
  • The core idea: "people similar to you also liked this."
  • The system doesn't need to know the item's details, just the interaction patterns like ratings, clicks, or purchase history.
Example: "Users who watched this movie also watched..." — built from the similarity in behavior across many users, not from the movie's content.
Turtle 🐢

What is Content-Based Filtering?

  • Recommends items based on the similarity of the items' own attributes or features.
  • The system looks at the characteristics you liked, then finds other items with similar traits.
  • It doesn't depend on other users' behavior, it focuses purely on the item's own content.
Example: you like action movies with a certain actor, so the system recommends other action movies with a similar genre or actor.

How does it all connect?

Unsupervised Learning is one of the machine learning approaches that underlies Recommendation Systems.

Within a Recommendation System, there are two main techniques: Collaborative Filtering (looking at the behavior of similar users) and Content-Based Filtering (looking at the similarity of item content/attributes).

Combining both makes recommendations more accurate and relevant!
